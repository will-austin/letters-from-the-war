A Soldier's Words Home

These letters, written during the Second World War, represent the correspondence between a young soldier and his mother back home. Each letter is a window into daily life during wartime—the mundane details, the hopes, the fears, and the unwavering connection to family that sustained so many through those difficult years.

The letters have been carefully preserved, scanned, and transcribed to share with future generations.

About This Archive

This digital archive contains letters written during World War II. Each letter has been carefully scanned at high resolution and transcribed using OCR technology, then manually verified for accuracy.

Some letters may contain redactions made by wartime censors. These have been preserved as historical artifacts.
